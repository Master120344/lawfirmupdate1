2. Information We Collect for Your Visa Needs

To effectively prepare and file H-2A and H-2B visa petitions and to provide comprehensive legal advice, we must collect various types of personal information. This happens when you interact with our Site, fill out forms (either online or paper), communicate with us by email, phone, or mail, and throughout our attorney client relationship.

2.1. Information from Client Employers (Petitioners)

If you are an employer seeking our services, we may collect information such as:

  • Your business's full legal name, structure (e.g., corporation, LLC, sole proprietorship), and federal Employer Identification Number (EIN).
  • Physical and mailing addresses for your business locations.
  • Contact details for key personnel, including names, titles, email addresses, and phone and fax numbers.
  • Information about your business operations, such as your industry, seasonal or temporary needs, and details about the job positions to be filled.
  • Documentation related to your business's legitimacy, financial standing (where required for the visa process, such as proof of ability to pay wages), and tax compliance.
  • Details of your recruitment efforts for U.S. workers, as required by the Department of Labor.

2.2. Information from or about Prospective Employees (Beneficiaries)

For individuals who are beneficiaries of visa petitions, we, through the employer client or directly with consent, may collect extensive personal information, including but not limited to:

  • Full legal name, including any previous names used.
  • Date and country of birth.
  • Gender and marital status.
  • Nationality and citizenship.
  • Current and past residential addresses.
  • Passport details, including passport number, issuance and expiration dates, and issuing country.
  • Travel history, especially to the United States.
  • Information about family members, including spouses and children, if they are included in or relevant to the visa application.
  • Educational background and qualifications.
  • Detailed work history and relevant experience for the offered position.
  • Contact information such as phone numbers and email addresses.
  • Photographs and other biometric information if required for visa applications (e.g., for DS-160 forms).
  • Information regarding previous immigration applications or history.
  • Answers to questions concerning admissibility to the United States, which may include health related information or criminal history, as required by U.S. immigration forms. This sensitive information is treated with the highest level of care.

2.3. Website Usage Data (Non Personally Identifiable)

When you visit our Site, we may automatically collect certain technical information that does not, by itself, identify you personally. This can include:

  • Your Internet Protocol (IP) address and general geographic location derived from it.
  • The type of browser and operating system you are using.
  • The dates and times you access our Site.
  • The specific pages you visit on our Site and the time spent on those pages.
  • The website you came from before visiting our Site (referring URL) and the website you visit after leaving our Site.
  • Information collected through cookies and similar tracking technologies, as detailed further in Section 5.

We collect this information to provide and improve our services, to comply with legal requirements, and to manage our client relationships effectively.

5. Cookies and Website Tracking Technologies

Our Site, www.kershawlawfirm.com, may use "cookies" and similar tracking technologies (like web beacons or pixels) to enhance your experience and gather information about visitors and visits to our Site.   • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the Site to function properly. They enable basic functionalities like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website. The Site cannot function properly without these cookies.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect information about how you use our Site, such as which pages you visit most often and if you receive error messages from web pages. This data helps us improve how our Site works. We may use third party analytics services like Google Analytics for this purpose.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies allow our Site to remember choices you make (such as your username, language, or the region you are in) and provide enhanced, more personal features.
  • Targeting or Advertising Cookies: We generally do not use targeting or advertising cookies that track your browsing habits to show you advertisements on other sites. If this practice changes, this policy will be updated, and appropriate consent mechanisms will be implemented.

5.2. Your Choices Regarding Cookies

Most web browsers are set to accept cookies by default. You can usually modify your browser setting to decline cookies if you prefer. If you choose to remove or reject cookies, please be aware that this could affect the availability and functionality of our Site. For more information about how to manage and delete cookies, visit your browser's help resources or sites like www.allaboutcookies.org.

Some analytics providers offer their own opt out mechanisms (e.g., Google Analytics Opt out Browser Add on).

8. Your Rights Regarding Your Personal Information

Depending on your geographical location and applicable data protection laws, you may have certain rights regarding the personal information we hold about you. These rights can include:

  • The Right to Access: You may have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you and to receive a copy of that information.
  • The Right to Rectification: You may have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate personal information or complete any incomplete personal information.
  • The Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Under certain conditions, you may have the right to request that we delete your personal information. This right is not absolute and may be limited by our legal and ethical obligations, such as file retention rules.
  • The Right to Restrict Processing: You may have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information under certain circumstances.
  • The Right to Object to Processing: You may have the right to object to our processing of your personal information under certain conditions, particularly for direct marketing purposes.
  • The Right to Data Portability: Under certain conditions, you may have the right to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine readable format and to have it transmitted to another controller.
  • The Right to Withdraw Consent: If our processing of your personal information is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
  • The Right to Opt Out of Marketing: You can ask us to stop sending you marketing communications at any time by following the unsubscribe links in any marketing message sent to you or by contacting us directly.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in the "Contact Us" section below. We will consider and act upon any request in accordance with applicable data protection laws. We may need to request specific information from you to help us confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your personal data (or to exercise any of your other rights).
